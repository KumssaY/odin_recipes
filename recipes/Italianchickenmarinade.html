Chicken Marinade

This Italian dressing chicken marinade is a super simple but delicious way to add flavor before grilling.

A chicken marinade is a flavorful mixture that is used to enhance the taste and tenderness of chicken. It typically consists of a combination of ingredients that work together to infuse the meat with delicious flavors. Common components of a chicken marinade include acidic elements like citrus juice, vinegar, or yogurt, which help to tenderize the chicken by breaking down its fibers. In addition, herbs and spices such as garlic, ginger, cumin, paprika, or oregano are often added to provide aromatic and savory notes. Sweeteners like honey or brown sugar may also be included to balance out the flavors. The chicken is typically submerged in the marinade and left to marinate for a period of time, allowing the flavors to penetrate the meat. This process not only adds taste but also helps to keep the chicken moist and juicy during cooking. The result is a succulent and well-seasoned chicken dish that is sure to delight the taste buds.

Directions

  1. In a shallow baking dish, whisk together salad dressing, garlic powder, and salt.
  2. Add chicken breasts to the dish and turn them to coat them in the marinade.
  3. Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 4 hours to overnight.
  4. Preheat an outdoor grill to high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  5. Remove the chicken from the marinade, shaking off any excess, and discard the remaining marinade.
  6. Place the chicken on the preheated grill and cook for about 7 to 8 minutes on each side, until it is no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear.
  7. To ensure the chicken is thoroughly cooked, use an instant-read thermometer inserted into the center, which should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).

Ingredients

  • 1 (16 ounce) bottle Italian-style salad dressing
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
