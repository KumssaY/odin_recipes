Marinated Grilld Pork Tenderloin

This marinated pork loin cooks up nicely on the grill. It tastes best when marinated for 24 hours.

A Marinated Grilled Pork Tenderloin is a flavorful and juicy dish made by marinating a pork tenderloin in a delicious blend of seasonings and then grilling it to perfection. The tenderloin is typically marinated in a mixture of ingredients such as soy sauce, garlic, ginger, honey, and various herbs and spices, allowing the flavors to penetrate the meat and enhance its taste. Once marinated, the pork tenderloin is grilled until it is nicely charred on the outside while remaining tender and moist on the inside. The result is a mouthwatering dish with a beautiful balance of savory and slightly sweet flavors, making it a delightful choice for any meat lover.

Directions

  1. Prepare marinade: Whisk together honey, soy sauce, oyster sauce, brown sugar, ginger, ketchup, garlic, parsley, onion powder, cayenne pepper, and cinnamon in a bowl.
  2. Pour the marinade into a resealable plastic bag.
  3. Place pork tenderloins in the bag, coat with marinade, remove excess air, and seal the bag.
  4. Marinate the pork in the refrigerator for 1 to 24 hours.
  5. Preheat the grill to medium heat and lightly oil the grate.
  6. Remove pork from the marinade, shaking off excess. Discard the remaining marinade.
  7. Grill the pork tenderloins for 20 to 30 minutes, turning occasionally, until no longer pink in the center.
  8. Ensure the pork reaches an internal temperature of at least 145 degrees F (63 degrees C) by using an instant-read thermometer.

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup honey
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 4 teaspoons minced fresh ginger root
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • ¼ teaspoon onion powder
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 (12 ounce) pork tenderloins
